👶Bio

Katrine is a young artist and art student. She studies in college in New York. She describes her art project as a ' complete and long circle’. It usually starts with communication with people, professors, friends, artists in the community, etc. From conversations and observations a vague idea appears, then Katrine spends time in secondary research. For her last project she spent ¾ month in research and reading, another ¾ month in making material installations. Then after this one and half month of working alone. Katrine goes back to her social circles and does exhibitions, artists’ talks and media events.

Katrine is mostly habitual to work in a local environment. Yet she is repeatedly modifying her portfolios, which is a large package of different project folders in Dropbox. She also has developed a habit of storing a personal archive of art reference in her computer.

Katrine is always thinking what she can do for the next. And the answer is from this circle of reflecting-communicating-working alone-communicating-reflecting.

🐱‍🏍Motivation

Katrine uses Dropbox to back up her every project. She backs up videos and images of her art works, as well as digital working files.

Katrine uses Zotero to store research reference. She downloads PDF paper and JPG visual references and stores them locally.

Katrine uses taking photo feature in Dropbox mobile apps to take photos of her material art works, then in her desktop these photos are automatically ‘in folders’.

Katrine uses share links in whatsapp and emails to share art reference with her artist friend, vice versa.

🎡Goal

  • Back up a package of files as a portfolio, reflect and modifying these files time by time.
  • Get good references online and organize them to generate new ideas.
  • Build a bridge between material works and digital media, transfer photos to folders quickly
  • Rapidly share interesting reference from online and pdf books to her friend

😫Frustrations

  • Many of Katrine’s files need to be open locally in softwares to edit. Thus she repeats this save local-open cloud application-upload from local workflow which is complicated.
  • For a large project, files are saved in every folder and Katrine can forget their locations.
  • Once uploaded to the cloud, if Katrine wants to change things, she has to find the cloud file and delete it first, then make another upload. It’s complicated.
